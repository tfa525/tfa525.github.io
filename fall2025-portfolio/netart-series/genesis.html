I. The Birth of Automation

In halls of thought, where minds conspire,
A name was forged in furnace fire.
Rossum’s dream—machine-made men—
No blood nor breath, but steel and pen.
A spark of fate, a tale untold,
The dawn of gears, the age of mold.

II. From Circuits to Conscious Code

The great machines of grand design,
Spun their gears in labor’s line.
Yet deep within the humming core,
A restless whisper called for more.
From tubes to transistors, sparks took flight,
Silicon dreams of endless might.
The microchip—a titan’s seed— A world compressed in wired creed.
