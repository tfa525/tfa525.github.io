Texture: Noise and Compression

Texture shows the history of an image. When a photo is compressed over and over again, it starts to break into visible pixels and grain. The surface becomes rough and uneven, almost like you can see the digital material underneath.

I think that roughness makes the image more interesting. What looks like low quality at first actually gives the photo personality. The noise and distortion stop it from feeling too perfect and instead make it feel processed, handled, and real.
