GLITCH INITATED

A glitch is a brief error that does not end up breaking the system. This can be caused by file corruption, compression issues, or data transfer errors. This often portrays pixilation, color distortion, frozen or repeated frames, as well as tearing lines. Artists have taken this "failure" and turned it into an aesthetic. The purpose is to use visual noise and a broken aesthetic to create art that is unpredictable and causes the feeling of instability.

MEMORY-FAILURE

These art pieces show the fading memory associated with glitch art as caused by corruption. The details become blurred. There is also a recognizable association too the repeated frames of a glitched system error. Details are blurring and disappearing. What appears as failure becomes expressive art. RGB color modes were used, then misaligned to create the system error glitch.
